stocks rallied friday, clinching weekly gains, as the u.s. economy added more jobs than expected in november.

the dow jones industrial average and s&p 500 each hit fresh all-time highs. the dow climbed about 118 points to 24,330. the s&p 500 advanced 14 points to 2,651. the nasdaq composite was up 27 points at 6,840.

the latest monthly jobs report signaled continued strength in the nations economy. employers added 228,000 jobs last month, exceeding estimates for a monthly gain of 200,000. wages ticked higher, while the unemployment rate remained steady at 4.1%.

global markets also got an early boost on news that the u.k. and european union agreed to brexit terms after a six-month negotiation.
