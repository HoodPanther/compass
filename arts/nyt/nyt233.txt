sydney, australia  lawmakers in australias house of representatives came ready on monday for a final debate on a bill to legalize same-sex marriage in the country.

but one member had a particularly important question prepared: a proposal to his long-term partner.

in a speech, tim wilson, 37, an openly gay member of parliament with the center-right liberal party, spoke emotionally of the struggles he and his partner, ryan bolger, had encountered as a gay couple.

this debate has been the soundtrack to our relationship, he said.

while they already considered themselves engaged, the rings they wore on their left hands, he said, were their answers to a question that they once could not ask.

so theres only one thing left to do, mr. wilson said, his voice choking. he turned to the public gallery, where mr. bolger was sitting. ryan patrick bolger, will you marry me?
